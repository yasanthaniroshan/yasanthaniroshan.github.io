A hands-on introduction to bare-metal programming on the Arduino Uno (**ATmega328p**) using pure C and the AVR-GCC toolchain — no Arduino libraries, no abstractions, just direct register manipulation and timer interrupts.

## Why bare metal?

Working at register level gives precise control over timers, interrupts, and peripherals, eliminates library overhead, and builds a much deeper understanding of how the microcontroller actually works. The repository walks through the toolchain setup and a series of progressively lower-level examples.
